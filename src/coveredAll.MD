The Cash-Secured Put (CSP) Runbook
This translates your 10 rules into a strict, executable system for Put Selling, locking in your new 90% capital requirement.
1. The 90% Liquidity Mandate (The Core Guardrail)
You must hold 90% of the total notional position size in cash or liquid equivalents for every put you sell. If you sell a put on a stock with a ₹1,000 strike and a 500 lot size (₹5,00,000 notional value), you must have ₹4,50,000 ready. This guarantees you will never face a forced broker liquidation during a flash crash.
2. Avoid Stock Selection Style Drift
Only sell puts on fundamentally strong, top-tier stocks (Nifty 100 or your deeply researched watchlist) that you genuinely want to own at a discount. Never chase abnormally high IV premiums on fundamentally weak or highly speculative mid-caps.
3. Define the Assignment Stop Before Entry

Before hitting "Sell," you must make a binary decision:
•	Plan A: I am happy to take delivery of these shares at this strike price.
•	Plan B: I will buy back the put and take the loss if the underlying spot price breaches [X] level.
You must know which plan you are executing before the trade goes live.
4. Never Average Down a Falling Knife
If a stock crashes toward your put strike, never sell more puts at a lower strike to collect extra premium and "average out" your position. Accept the assignment or take the stop loss.
5. Nail Down Theta Profits Early
If a fast IV crush or a sudden market rally allows you to capture 50% to 70% of your maximum premium within the first few days, buy the put back and close the trade. Do not hold a put for 20 more days just to squeeze out the final few rupees of premium while exposing your 90% capital buffer to tail-end risk.
6. Never Let a "Won" Put Turn Red
If your short put is deeply in profit and the stock starts breaking critical support levels (like the EMA 50), close the trade flat or with a small gain. You protect your capital buffer immediately.
7. Scale Sizing with Market Regimes
Trade your maximum allowable size (while respecting the 90% rule) during stable, bullish, or sideways markets where you are trading your best. If the VIX spikes above 18 and the broader market trend turns bearish, aggressively scale down your lot sizes or halt put selling entirely.
8. Trail Your Worst-Case Scenario
As the underlying stock moves further away from your strike price and the trade improves, tighten your mental stop. If the put's value drops to 15% of what you sold it for, set a hard buy-to-close order so that a sudden reversal cannot harm you.
